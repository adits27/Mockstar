# Scoring System

MockStar produces two independent scores per session — a computer vision confidence score derived from video, and a multi-dimensional interview score generated by Gemini — which are combined into a single overall result.

---

## 1. CV Confidence Score (0 – 100)

Computed per turn by the OpenCV gaze pipeline, then averaged across all turns at session end.

### Components

| Component | Max Points | How it's calculated |
|---|---|---|
| Gaze on camera | 40 | `gaze_on_camera_pct × 40` (linear) |
| Gaze-away frequency | 30 | ≤ 4 events/min → **30 pts** · ≤ 8/min → **20 pts** · ≤ 14/min → **10 pts** · > 14/min → **0 pts** |
| Avg gaze-away duration | 20 | ≤ 1.0 s → **20 pts** · ≤ 2.5 s → **12 pts** · ≤ 5.0 s → **5 pts** · > 5.0 s → **0 pts** |
| Face visibility | 10 | `face_detected_pct × 10` (linear) |
| **Total** | **100** | |

### Labels

| Score | Label |
|---|---|
| ≥ 75 | High |
| 50 – 74 | Medium |
| < 50 | Low |

### How gaze is classified
Each video frame is sampled at 100 ms intervals. The OpenCV Haar cascade detects the face and eyes; the iris centre is estimated as the darkest pixel within each eye bounding box. A normalised offset > ±0.15 from centre is classified as a gaze-away event.

---

## 2. Gemini Interview Score (1 – 10 per dimension)

Gemini 2.5 Flash evaluates the full conversation transcript — every question and answer — using an anchored rubric.

### Scale Anchors

| Band | Range | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Below expectations | 1 – 4 | Significant gaps; vague or off-topic |
| Meets basic expectations | 5 – 6 | Adequate but room for improvement |
| Strong performance | 7 – 8 | Clear, specific, relevant — minor refinements needed |
| Exceptional | 9 – 10 | Rare; outstanding depth, precision, and relevance |

### Dimensions

| Dimension | What it measures | Video required? |
|---|---|---|
| `answer_relevance` | Did the answer directly address what was asked? | No |
| `experience_articulation` | Were examples specific, credible, and well-described? | No |
| `industry_fit` | Appropriate terminology and domain awareness? | No |
| `clarity_and_structure` | Well-organised and easy to follow? | No |
| `filler_word_usage` | Delivery polish — 10 = no fillers; only penalised for heavy, disruptive usage | No |
| `eye_contact_and_presence` | Camera focus and projected confidence, informed by the CV confidence score above | **Yes** |

### Overall Score

Gemini returns a weighted float average across all present dimensions. The effective weighting is approximately **70 % content/delivery, 30 % presence** when video is provided. The `eye_contact_and_presence` dimension is omitted entirely when no video was uploaded.

**Why 70 / 30?**
Content and delivery account for 70 % because they are the primary signal in any interview evaluation — what a candidate says, how clearly they say it, and whether their experience is relevant are what interviewers ultimately hire on. Presence matters, but it is a modifier rather than the core criterion.

The 30 % cap on video serves two additional purposes. First, video is optional in MockStar — audio analysis always runs, video does not. Capping presence at 30 % ensures the overall score remains meaningful and comparable even when no video is provided. Second, the CV pipeline operates on webcam footage in uncontrolled environments: poor lighting, an off-centre camera, or a small laptop screen can depress the confidence score for reasons unrelated to actual interview performance. A 30 % ceiling limits the blast radius of these environmental factors on the final result.

---

## Explicit Exclusions

| Factor | Reason |
|---|---|
| Speaking pace (WPM) | Penalises non-native speakers and natural cadence variation without reflecting answer quality |
| Documented communication differences | Stammer, accent, etc. are passed to Gemini with an explicit instruction not to penalise them |
